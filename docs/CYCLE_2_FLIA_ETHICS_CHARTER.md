## 1. CORE ETHICAL PRINCIPLES

### Principle 1: Human Accountability
All legal decisions remain the responsibility of human judges and lawyers. **FLIA is advisory only.**

**Implementation:**
- No FLIA output is binding
- All outputs require human review before use
- Judges maintain final authority
- Lawyers retain professional responsibility

### Principle 2: Transparency
FLIA outputs must be explainable, logged, and traceable to inputs and model versions.

**Implementation:**
- Every FLIA action logged with input, reasoning, and output
- Audit trail accessible to all parties
- Model versions tracked and versioned
- Methodology documented for scrutiny

### Principle 3: Fairness & Bias Mitigation
FLIA must not use protected characteristics as risk signals and must be regularly audited for bias.

**Implementation:**
- No use of race, gender, disability, national origin in risk indicators
- Quarterly bias audits required
- External review for fairness assessment
- Remediation process for detected bias

### Principle 4: Confidentiality & Privilege
Client data processed by FLIA must be protected with strong security.

**Implementation:**
- Encryption at rest and in transit
- Role-based access control
- Legal privilege protection
- Data minimization (only necessary data)
- Secure deletion protocols

### Principle 5: Due Process & Rights
Presumption of innocence, right to counsel, and right to appeal are never overridden.

**Implementation:**
- No guilt predictions
- No sentencing recommendations
- No profiling of defendants
- Full rights protections in UI/UX
- Right to challenge FLIA outputs

### Principle 6: No Autonomous Adjudication
FLIA must not issue verdicts, sentencing recommendations, or binding legal conclusions.

**Implementation:**
- Prohibited: guilt/innocence determination
- Prohibited: sentencing suggestions
- Prohibited: auto-case outcomes
- Approved: analysis, flagging, research assistance

## 2. GOVERNANCE STRUCTURE

### A) FLIA Ethics & Governance Committee

**Composition:**
- Senior judge (chair)
- Senior lawyer
- Technologist / AI expert
- Ethicist / philosopher
- Compliance officer

**Responsibilities:**
- Approve use cases
- Review incidents and misuse
- Oversee bias and performance audits
- Update policies as law evolves
- Monitor constitutional alignment

**Meetings:** Monthly minimum

### B) Policy Framework

**Approved Uses:**
- Evidence analysis and integrity reporting
- Legal research and precedent surfacing
- Procedural requirement checking
- Advisory risk flagging (never guilt)
- Drafting assistance with human review

**Prohibited Uses:**
- Guilt or innocence determination
- Sentencing recommendations
- Automated case outcomes
- Psychological profiling
- Racial, gender, or disability profiling
- Sentencing guidelines override

**Human-in-the-Loop:**
- All outputs reviewed before use in court
- All filings disclosing FLIA use
- All parties can request FLIA disclosure

### C) Audit & Incident Response

**Audit Processes:**
- Quarterly performance review
- Quarterly bias audit
- Annual external security audit
- Annual ethics audit

**Incident Response:**
- Defined process for investigating errors or misuse
- Ability to suspend or restrict features if risk detected
- Public disclosure of incidents (unless sealed)
- Remediation documentation

## 3. OPERATIONAL SAFEGUARDS

### A) Mandatory Disclaimers

Every FLIA output must include:
```
⚠️  ADVISORY ONLY
This is analysis for judicial consideration, not a determination of guilt.
All findings require human verification and judgment.
FLIA does not determine guilt, innocence, or sentencing.
```

### B) Confidence Scoring

All outputs include confidence levels (0.0–1.0) with explicit caveats:
- "This finding has 0.87 confidence; human verification required."
- Uncertainty flags for edge cases
- Limitations boxes on all reports

### C) Regular Retraining & Evaluation

- Quarterly model performance review
- Annual retraining on jurisdiction-specific updates
- External expert review of outputs
- User feedback integration

### D) External Alignment

- Compliance with state AI court guidelines
- Alignment with ABA technology standards
- Regular review against judicial ethics opinions
- Incorporation of best practices
